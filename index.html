Medical Insurance Cost Analysis (Academic Project):

For an insurance company to be profitable, it must collect a greater amount in subscription fees than it spends on medical care for its beneficiaries. As a result, insurers invest significant resources in improving their ability to predict medical expenses. Medical expenses are hard to estimate because the most costly conditions are rare and appear random. Yet, some conditions affect certain segments of the population more frequently. Smokers are more likely to develop lung cancer as opposed to non-smokers, and obese people are more likely to develop heart disease. Using patient data, this project intends to estimate medical expenses for such distinct population segments. These estimates can be used for the calculation of actuarial tables which can determine whether the price of premiums will be higher or lower based on the expected treatment costs.

Besides this, We also tried to see if we can classify whether a person is a smoker or not based solely on the health parameters.
